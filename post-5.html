My Wish

2026-03-08

The Nation I Desire

I want our nation to be the most beautiful nation in the world. I do not wish for it to be the most powerful or wealthy. Because I have felt the pain of being invaded by others, I do not want my nation to invade others. It is enough if our wealth is sufficient to make our lives abundant and our strength is enough to repel foreign invasions. The only thing I desire to possess without limit is the power of a high culture. This is because the power of culture makes us happy and, furthermore, brings happiness to others. What humanity lacks today is neither military might nor economic power.

No matter how advanced natural science becomes, it is welcome; however, for humanity as a whole, the current state of science is already sufficient for a comfortable life. The fundamental reason humanity is unhappy today is a lack of benevolence (In), righteousness (Ui), compassion, and love. If these qualities of the heart are developed, the current material power would be enough for all two billion people to live in peace.

It is only culture that cultivates this spirit of humanity. I do not want our nation to be a copycat of others; instead, I want it to become the source, the goal, and the model of this high and new culture. I desire that true world peace be realized in the world through us, starting from our nation.

I believe this is the ideal of our founding father, Dangun: Hongik Ingan (To broadly benefit humanity). I also believe that the talent, spirit, and past hardships of our people are sufficient to achieve this mission, that our geographical location and conditions are favorable, and that this era in which we are rebuilding our nation is the right time. Can we not see the day when our people will step onto the world stage as lead actors? To accomplish this, what we must do is establish a political system that ensures freedom of thought and perfect our national education. This is why I emphasized a nation of freedom and the importance of education.

The mission of a people to build the highest culture can be summarized in one word: making everyone a Saint. Every Korean person should be trusted and respected wherever they go.

While our enemies oppressed us, we cultivated a spirit of fierce struggle out of hatred and resentment. But since the enemy has retreated, it is time to discard the struggle of hatred and focus on the construction of harmony. A household that feuds will fall, and a nation that fights within itself will perish. Hatred and struggle among compatriots are signs of ruin.

A peaceful energy must shine from our countenances. A warm spring breeze must always blow through our land. Through this, we shall endure. Each member of our nation, tasked with becoming a model for humanity through the highest culture, must not be a selfish individualist. We claim individual freedom to the extreme, but it is not the freedom used by beasts to merely fill their own bellies; it is the freedom used to make one’s family, neighbors, and fellow citizens live well.

It is not the freedom to pluck flowers in a park, but the freedom to plant flowers in a park. We are not people who take from others or rely on their virtue; we find joy in giving to our neighbors and compatriots. This is what we call a Seonbi (scholar-official) or a refined person. Therefore, we are not lazy but diligent. A head of a household who loves his wife and children cannot help but be diligent. It is for the sake of giving limitlessly. I step forward for difficult tasks out of love for my compatriots, and I offer pleasant things to others for the sake of those I love. This is the "Virtue of Benevolence and Generosity" (In-hu-ji-deok) that our ancestors cherished.

By doing so, the mountains of our nation will be lush with forests, the fields will overflow with grains and fruits, and our villages and cities will be clean, prosperous, and peaceful. Thus, our compatriots—the Korean people, both men and women—will always have peace on their faces and emit the fragrance of virtue from their bodies. Such a nation cannot be unhappy even if it tries, and it cannot perish even if it wishes to. National happiness never comes from class struggle, nor does individual happiness come from selfishness. Class struggle breeds endless conflict that dries up the blood of the land, and if I harm others out of selfishness, the world will harm me out of selfishness; this is a law where one gains little and loses much. The retribution Japan recently suffered is the best example of this internationally and nationally. What I have said above is a sketch of the appearance of the new nation I desire.

My fellow compatriots! How wonderful would it be if such a nation were realized? How satisfied would we be to leave such a country to our descendants? In the past, Gija of the Han lands yearned for our country and came here; even Confucius wished to live among our people, calling us a nation that loves benevolence. If it was so in the past, shall we not make all of humanity yearn for our culture in the future?

I believe that through our strength, especially through the power of education, this will surely be achieved. If all the young men and women of our nation hold this heart, how can it not be accomplished!

I was once engaged in education in Hwanghae Province, and this was exactly what I hoped for through education. Now that I am over seventy, I do not have much time left to personally engage in national education, but I cannot help but pray that the educators and students of this world will have a great change of heart.

— 1947, outside Saetmun. Paikbum Kim Koo.
